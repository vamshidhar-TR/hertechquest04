🔔 Return-to-Return Variance Alerts — Voice Workflow

Load last year + this year, fill in the return, and your app compares the two as you work — when a number is way off (or a form is missing) it speaks up. If you want to know more, you can ask CoCounsel a follow-up.

The tax pro Data you upload Your app (compare + voice) CoCounsel (optional follow-up)
Who does what: the numbers come from the two returns you upload — last year is the "answer key." Your app does the comparison (it's just math) and speaks the alert. CoCounsel is not the source of the data — it's the optional brain you ask after an alert, when you want the tax rule or an explanation. Most of the time the alert alone is enough and the tax pro just fixes the number.

Prior art — does this already exist?

Yes, partly — and that's worth knowing before you build. Year-over-year comparison is a standard US tax practice (even the IRS audit manual compares a return to prior years), and a few tools already do the compare-and-flag part. Use these as reference for what's table stakes vs. what's your differentiator.

ToolWhat it doesHow close to UC5
Juno TaxAI review compares to the prior year and surfaces "what changed" + missing/mismatched forms, so reviewers see real exceptions instead of re-checking every lineClosest — does steps 2–3 on screen
CCH Axcess Expert AIAnomaly detection, missing-form flagging, audit-risk flags embedded in the workflowStrong — proactive flags, on screen
Drake Tax Comparison SheetCarries prior-year numbers forward and lays out this-year-vs-last side by side (the plain baseline)Baseline — passive sheet you read
Your differentiator = voice, not the comparison. Every tool above makes you look at a screen to find the variance. None speak the alert, and none let you ask a voice follow-up. UC5's edge is hands-free delivery: the app says the anomaly out loud while you fill the return, and you ask CoCounsel back by voice for a cited answer. Build that part — the comparison itself is table stakes.

Note: a fixed threshold like "20% off" is a tunable demo choice, not an industry standard — the IRS audit manual references ~5%, and commercial tools generally leave the judgment to the preparer.
